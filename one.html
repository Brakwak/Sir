Personal Hygiene

What is Personal Hygiene?

Many a time, learners define Personal Hygiene as the ways of keeping the body clean and healthy. The ending says keeping the body clean and healthy right?

. .

That is not wrong, neither is it completely right. The body in question here lives in an environment so if the body is clean but its environs is dirty, is that Healthy Living?The body would not live healthy since its environs is unhealthy.

With that being said, we can conclude by saying that PERSONAL HYGIENE REFERS TO THE WAYS OF KEEPING THE BODY AND IT'S ENVIRONS CLEAN AND HEALTHY.

How Can We Keep Our Body and Its Environs Clean and Healthy?

> Hand Washing: Washing of hands with soap and under running water for at least 20 seconds frequently. This helps to remove dirt, bacteria, and viruses that can cause illness.

The hand is a part of the body that is frequently used either voluntary or involuntary on all other parts of the body, even on delecate parts (the private parts), hence, it oughts to be clean and healthy always so as to avoid transmission of infections.

NOT FORGETTING If the hand can completely be free from dirt, virus or bacteria, its environs (areas where the hand touches like door handles, chairs, walls and many more) should be sanitized regularly.

.

> Oral Hygiene: Oral Hygiene involves practices that ensures strong teeth, healthy gum and good breath, thus, brushing of the teeth twice a day, flossing daily, avoiding the intake of too much sweets, too hot or too cold foods. This prevents cavities [whole in tooth], gum disease [gingivities], and bad breath [halitosis].

Why Is Personal Hygiene so Important?

Brush your teeth twice a day and floss daily. This prevents cavities, gum disease, and bad breath.

Food Hygiene

Why is Food Hygiene Important?

Food hygiene involves handling, preparing, and storing food in a way that prevents foodborne illnesses. It ensures that food is safe to eat.

Proper Food Storage

Store food at the right temperature. Refrigerate perishable foods and keep dry foods in sealed containers to prevent contamination.

Cooking Food Safely

Cook food to the correct temperature to kill harmful bacteria. Use a food thermometer to check if food is properly cooked.

Daily Routine

Morning Routine

Start your day with a refreshing shower, brush your teeth, and have a healthy breakfast to kickstart your day.

Afternoon Routine

Wash your hands before lunch, stay hydrated, and take short breaks to stay active.

Evening Routine

Wind down your day by brushing your teeth, washing your face, and preparing for a good night's sleep.

Healthy Habits

Exercise Regularly

Engage in physical activities like sports, running, or even walking to keep your body fit and healthy.

Eat Balanced Meals

Include a variety of foods in your diet to get all the necessary nutrients. Eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and proteins.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water throughout the day to keep your body hydrated and functioning properly.

Safety Tips

Use Hand Sanitizer

When you can't wash your hands, use hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol to kill germs.

Disinfect Surfaces

Regularly clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces like doorknobs, light switches, and mobile phones.

Stay Home if Unwell

If you're feeling sick, stay home to prevent spreading illness to others.
